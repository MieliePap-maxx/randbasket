# RandBasket Monetisation Roadmap

## Current decision: free-first launch

RandBasket will launch as a completely free product while it builds trust, catalogue coverage and an active user base.

Do not add a paywall, subscription requirement or sponsored ranking during the initial traction phase.

The following remain free:

- Product search and individual price comparisons.
- Unit-price comparisons.
- Building, saving and sharing baskets.
- Full basket comparisons.
- Best-retailer and basket-total calculations.
- Estimated savings.
- Catalogue and specials browsing.
- Product favourites when introduced.
- Basic price alerts when introduced.
- Location-aware results.

## Deferred consumer monetisation

### Smart Basket Compare

Potential future one-time lifetime purchase:

- Full basket comparison across retailers.
- Cheapest complete-retailer basket.
- Split-store basket optimisation.
- Exact estimated saving against the user's usual or selected retailer.
- Missing-product and substitution guidance.

Possible future pricing:

- Launch offer: R49.99.
- Standard price: R99.99.
- Higher price only after materially stronger optimisation is available.

This feature is intentionally free during the traction phase.

### Product Price Alerts

Potential future subscription:

- Follow specific or generic products.
- Target-price alerts.
- Alerts for prices below the observed normal price.
- New catalogue-special notifications.
- Expiring-special reminders.
- Historical price charts and deal-quality ratings.
- Automatic weekly basket monitoring.

Possible future pricing:

- R19.99 monthly.
- R149.99 annually.

Alerts should only become paid after RandBasket has reliable price history, dependable notifications and enough catalogue coverage to provide recurring value.

## Deferred business monetisation

### Location-aware sponsored placements

Companies may eventually pay to showcase relevant products or catalogues by broad location, retailer, category and campaign date.

Required trust rules:

- Every placement must be clearly labelled `Sponsored`, `Promoted` or `Paid placement`.
- Payment must never influence organic cheapest-price, best-unit-price or recommended-basket calculations.
- Sponsored products must appear in a separate placement area.
- Do not sell precise user locations or individual user identities.
- Location targeting should use broad geographic groups with minimum audience thresholds.
- Campaign reporting may include aggregated impressions, clicks, add-to-basket actions and approximate region.

Possible future commercial models:

- Fixed local, provincial or national campaigns.
- Cost per qualified retailer click.
- Sponsored catalogue placement.
- Clearly labelled brand campaigns.

## Conditions before monetising

Revisit monetisation only after RandBasket has evidence of:

- Reliable daily or weekly active usage.
- Strong search and catalogue coverage.
- Accurate basket comparisons.
- Reliable price freshness and special validity.
- Repeat basket usage.
- Enough price history to identify genuine bargains.
- User demand for automatic alerts or advanced optimisation.
- Clear analytics that respect the privacy policy.

## Launch principle

The immediate goal is adoption and trust, not short-term revenue.

RandBasket should first become useful enough that people regularly search, build baskets, compare totals and recommend it to others. Monetisation should later charge for additional automation, intelligence or commercial exposure without weakening the free comparison experience.
