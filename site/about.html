01

Why we built RandBasket

Finding a grocery price is easy. Deciding whether it represents good value is harder. Retailers often sell similar products in different sizes, varieties and promotional formats, making a quick comparison surprisingly confusing.

RandBasket brings those choices into one calmer view. The goal is to help shoppers compare like with like, understand the available information and make their own decision with confidence.

02

The problem we are solving

A lower shelf price does not always mean a lower cost for the same quantity. A 250 g pack and a 500 g pack cannot be compared fairly by looking only at their totals. The same is true for litres, millilitres, multipacks and products sold per item.

RandBasket uses product names, pack details and comparable units where available. It also keeps the original retailer and product context visible so shoppers can check the final choice.

03

Our principles

  • Make the important number easy to see.
  • Compare equivalent measurements whenever the source information allows it.
  • Be honest when products or sizes are not directly equivalent.
  • Keep the experience simple enough to use during a real shopping decision.
  • Correct catalogue problems when users or retailers bring them to our attention.

04

Independent from the retailers

RandBasket is not Pick n Pay, Checkers, Woolworths, SPAR or Makro, and it is not presented as an official service of any retailer. Retailer names and product information are used to identify and compare the choices available to shoppers.

Retailers remain responsible for their products, availability, checkout prices, fulfilment and customer service. RandBasket does not complete purchases or deliveries.

05

The road ahead

RandBasket is growing. The work ahead includes broader catalogue coverage, better local pricing, more reliable product matching and clearer explanations when a comparison is uncertain.

User corrections and suggestions are part of that process. If something is confusing or wrong, we want to know.
